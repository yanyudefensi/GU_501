Project Introduction

Written in Sep 5th, 2021 by Jacek

Trump wins the 2016 president election of United State, which is out of many famous media expectations. With a completed data collection, integration mechanism and powerful data prediction model built on past president elections, the famous media such as New York Times should predict a reliable prediction of the new president.

Of course, we know that nothing is 100% confirmed in prediction. However, the New York Times strongly believe that Trump 15% probability of winning the election (based on 2016 November prediction). Other famous medias do similar predictions. They regard that Trump’s victory is a small probability event. However, the fact is that Trump received 304 electoral votes and Clinton got 227.

That’s a great difference between the last result and the prediction which cannot be explained by model error. It must be a huge data bias during the whole data cycle. I’m interested in this data bias and want to find out where is the problem.

To answer my question, I plan to investigate past president election data first. And after that, I focus on 2016 president election and try to analyze what part of political groups be underestimated by the famous media. At last, I put forward my personal consideration of this data bias and some approaches to avoid similar terrible mistakes.

10 Related Question

Here are ten-related sub-problems. Answer these questions can help us know more about president election and are more likely to reach my research target.


  1. What is the difference between earlier president election and the 2016 president election?

  2. Why the media's poll predictions and the final election results differ greatly?

  3. Is it the supporter of Trump the silent majority?

  4. What is the data characteristic of Trump’s supporter?

  5. During the election, what is the text feature of Trump in twitter?

  6. Looking at the president election data of United State from 1976 to 2016, what are key factors of the president election?

  7. Is the problem with the media's wrong prediction of the presidential election in the data collection, cleaning, or other parts?

  8. Swing state votes are the most important to fight for, what kind of characteristics are presented in the data?

  9. Does Trump's election experience have anything in common with presidents who have successfully reversed elections throughout history?

  10. What can we learn from the wrong prediction of president election raised by data bias?
